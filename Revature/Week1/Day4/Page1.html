Space Travel

Through the Wormhole

Everyone has seen a science fiction movie where they use wormholes for travel across large distances. Even though these wormholes may not be purely science fiction, it is not just as simple as finding one and flying into it. There are a few key issues with traveling into a wormhole.

Potential obstacles

Problem Explanation
Size Wormholes at this time are only predicted to exist on a microscopic level, though some may have expanded over time.
Stability The predicted wormholes appear to collapse too quickly to be useful for travel.
Exotic Matter It appears exotic matter would be required to stabilize a wormhole, and none has been physically found and has only been seen in the behavior of certain vacuum states.
Human Travel It appears as though even if a wormhole could be stabilized, it may not be safe for humans to travel through.
Location Wormhole destinations may not be predictable, and could also lead to separate universe without much control over where that destination is.

Time Travel

It is speculated by some scientists that wormholes could possibly be used for time travel, but there does not seem to be a consensus on this. Some scientists believe it would be impossible to use a wormhole for time travel. Regardless of this fact, one thing seems to be certain: using wormholes for any kind of travel is far beyond our reach currently.
